Frequently Asked Questions

How does AfetNet work offline?

AfetNet uses Bluetooth mesh networking to communicate with nearby devices when internet is unavailable. All data is stored locally on your device and shared only with nearby users.

Is the Early Warning System (EEW) reliable?

The EEW system is experimental and should not be your primary source of earthquake warnings. Always follow official sources like AFAD and Kandilli. False alarms and missed warnings are possible.

How do I share logs for debugging?

In the app, go to Settings → Support → Share Logs. This will generate a debug report that you can email to our support team.

Can I delete all my data?

Yes. Go to Settings → "Delete All My Data" to completely clear all local data from your device. This action cannot be undone.

Does AfetNet collect personal information?

AfetNet stores data locally on your device and only shares messages temporarily with nearby devices via P2P. We don't collect personal information for tracking purposes. See our Privacy Policy for details.

Why do I need location permission?

Location permission is optional but recommended for help requests. It allows you to share your approximate location when requesting help during emergencies.

Why do I need Bluetooth permission?

Bluetooth is essential for P2P mesh networking. It allows AfetNet to communicate with nearby devices and relay emergency information when internet is unavailable.

How can I export my data?

Go to Settings → Data Export to export your help requests, resources, and damage reports in CSV or GeoJSON format.

Bug Reports

If you encounter a bug or unexpected behavior:

  1. Try restarting the app
  2. Check if you're running the latest version
  3. Gather relevant information (device model, OS version, steps to reproduce)
  4. Use the in-app "Share Logs" feature
  5. Email the logs to support@afetnet.org
